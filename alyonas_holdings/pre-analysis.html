⚖️ Investor Rights vs Operational Control

✅ What Investors CAN Do

  • Receive financial statements
  • Earn declared dividends
  • Participate in exit decisions
  • Receive fraud/misuse protection
  • Vote on major transactions

❌ What Investors CANNOT Do

  • Manage daily operations
  • Hire or remove staff
  • Control business strategy
  • Direct capital allocation
  • Run the business
Key Point: Investors are passive equity holders. Founders maintain full operational control and strategic decision-making authority. This ensures stable, focused business management.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who manages the capital after investment?

Founders manage capital allocation per the strategic plan. Investors receive quarterly financial summaries and annual detailed reviews.

Q: Can I exit early if needed?

Not before the lock-in period. After that, you can negotiate a secondary sale with founders or find replacement investors. No forced exit mechanism.

Q: What if the company loses value?

Share values decrease proportionally. This is equity investment risk. Mitigation: diversified business portfolio, founder expertise, regular reporting, and governance structure.

Q: When do I receive returns?

Three ways: (1) Optional dividends (founder-decided), (2) Share appreciation (realized on exit), (3) Exit event (buyback/acquisition/IPO typically in 3-5+ years).

Q: What about tax implications?

Consult a tax advisor. Capital gains, dividend income, and exit proceeds have tax implications based on your jurisdiction and investment type.

  • Dividend Policy: When & how dividends distributed
  • Board Composition: Investor seat on board?

Operational Setup Items

  • Reporting Schedule: Quarterly, semi-annual, or annual?
  • Exit Timeline: Year 5, 7, or 10 target exit?
  • ROFR Terms: When can investors sell shares?
  • Buyback Triggers: At what valuation multiple?
